# Merge Bloom -- Improvement Plan

**Date:** 2026-03-30
**Purpose:** Transform Merge Bloom from a functional merge game into a cozy, beautiful, Sanrio-inspired experience that rivals Travel Town in feel while being completely ad-free and pressure-free.
**Target audience:** Alex's girlfriend -- loves Sanrio characters, Cinnamoroll, and Travel Town.

---

## Part 1: Competitive Research

### 1.1 Travel Town (The Girlfriend's Favourite)

**Core Loop:**
- Merge-2 system (combine two identical items to level up) -- considered the best merge-2 engine in the market
- Generators produce tier-1 items when tapped, consuming energy
- Items merge into higher tiers to complete "orders" (quests) that advance the town
- Board returns to 20-25% empty space between sessions so players jump straight in
- Unique chain types: nestled chains (generator created mid-chain), combined chains (merge items from two chains), and no-energy generators (spawn items after cooldown)
- Power Boost toggle: 2x energy for chance at higher-tier spawns -- creates a "fast feels normal" psychological hook

**Visual Style:**
- Bright, colourful, cheerful cartoon aesthetic
- Detailed hand-drawn items with smooth animations
- Lush environments: forests, lakes, market squares, cozy homes
- Building upgrades visually transform -- watching a rundown area become a restored village is deeply satisfying
- Every corner feels "full of personality"

**Progression:**
- New merge chains unlock as levels increase
- Orders get progressively more complex
- Building restoration provides long-term visual goals
- Events give constant reasons to return (seasonal, limited-time)
- Daily tasks (3 per day) provide session structure

**Monetization (what to avoid):**
- Energy system is the primary frustration -- capped at 100, refills slowly (1 per ~2 minutes)
- Higher levels deliberately stall progression to push gem/energy spending
- Diamond purchases offer poor value for money
- Board space is deliberately constrained -- storage box costs diamonds to expand
- Players report the game becomes "too tedious and slow" at higher levels

**What players love:**
- Easy to learn, instantly satisfying
- Beautiful visual transformations as you progress
- "Just one more merge" flow state
- Variety of items (500+)
- Minimal ads compared to competitors

**What players hate:**
- Energy walls at higher levels
- Forced spending to maintain pace
- Small board with no room for failed items
- Expensive storage expansion
- Inconsistent order difficulty

**Key takeaway for Merge Bloom:** Copy the merge feel and visual satisfaction. Ditch the energy system entirely (already done). Our unlimited-gems, zero-pressure approach is the exact antidote to every Travel Town complaint.

### 1.2 Merge Mansion (The Story-Driven One)

**Core Loop:**
- Merge-2 items to clean up and renovate different areas of a mansion
- Items include tools, furniture, plants (e.g. two knives become pruning shears)
- Each area unlocks successively with XP from completed tasks
- Time-management elements: some tasks take real time to complete
- Inventory management is strategic -- immediate merges vs long-term planning

**Narrative Hook:**
- Grandma Ursula hands Maddie keys to a condemned mansion
- Renovation reveals family mysteries
- Story unfolds as new areas unlock -- each area is a chapter
- The mystery element keeps players curious about what happens next
- Viral marketing: intentionally "weird" and dramatic ads drove $120M+ annual revenue

**Visual Style:**
- Warm, inviting mansion environments
- Items are realistic but stylised
- Each area has a distinct personality
- Visual progression from dilapidated to beautiful is the primary reward

**Progression:**
- Area-based: each zone is a self-contained renovation project
- Story reveals are the carrot -- players merge to find out what happens next
- Complexity increases gradually across areas

**Monetization (what to avoid):**
- Energy gates between play sessions
- Premium currency for speeding up timed tasks
- Limited board space pushes spending

**What players love:**
- Mystery story makes merging feel purposeful
- Forgiving pace -- "merge at your own pace with no hard failures"
- Satisfaction of finally creating a needed item after extended effort
- Renovation transformation visuals

**What players hate:**
- Slow progression at higher levels
- Unclear merge requirements
- Energy limitations

**Key takeaway for Merge Bloom:** Story transforms merging from an idle activity into a journey. Our story beats system is already good -- we should expand it with more character, warmth, and a mascot who reacts to your progress.

### 1.3 Merge Dragons (The OG)

**Core Loop:**
- Merge-3 or merge-5 (not merge-2) -- matching sets of 3 identical items or 5 for bonus
- Merge items AND dragons -- dragons are workers that harvest resources
- Camp (persistent world) + levels (bite-sized challenges) -- dual-mode structure
- Land healing mechanic: merging items heals "dead land" on the map
- $780M+ all-time revenue -- the genre benchmark

**What It Pioneered:**
- Creator Ray Mazza combined The Sims Social with Triple Town's merge mechanic
- Introduced expansive worlds and varied elements beyond simple merging
- Established merge chains as a core retention loop
- Proved the merge genre could sustain massive revenue

**Visual Style:**
- Fantasy/magical aesthetic with dragons
- Vibrant colours, detailed environments
- Camp grows into a sprawling kingdom over time

**Progression:**
- Level-based challenges provide structured play
- Camp provides open-ended, relaxing merge gameplay
- Dragon collection is the meta-progression layer
- Many dragon types -- collection drive keeps players engaged
- Events provide premium dragons without mandatory spending

**What players love:**
- "Soothing game and great time killer"
- Lovely visuals
- Dual mode (structured levels + open camp)
- Dragon variety and collection
- Events where premium items are earnable

**What players hate:**
- Overwhelming complexity at later stages
- Ads and monetisation push
- Performance issues on older devices

**Key takeaway for Merge Bloom:** The dual-mode structure (focused challenges + open-ended play) is compelling. A "daily challenge" mode with a small constrained board would add variety without complexity.

## Part 2: Sanrio/Kawaii Aesthetic Direction

### 2.1 Cinnamoroll Design Principles

Cinnamoroll is the #1 Sanrio character (overtook Hello Kitty). His design principles are the north star for Merge Bloom's visual language:

- **Soft, rounded everything** -- no sharp corners, no hard edges
- **Emotionally readable** -- unlike Hello Kitty's blank expression, Cinnamoroll looks like he "feels things"
- **Comfort, kindness, warmth** -- the design itself signals safety
- **Pastel colour dominance** -- light blue, pale pink, soft lavender
- **Simple but expressive** -- minimal detail, maximum charm

### 2.2 Sanrio Character Design Language

Core rules from Sanrio's visual language that should inform our mascot and UI:

**Proportions:**
- Large head relative to body (baby-like proportions)
- Protruding forehead, large eyes, chubby cheeks
- Designed to trigger nurturing/affectionate response

**Facial Features:**
- Large, simple eyes -- the primary expression tool
- Minimal or no mouth in neutral state
- Asymmetrical expressions for charm
- "Clean design" ensures recognition at any size (even tiny icons)
- Limited expressions leave room for viewer projection

**Colour Philosophy:**
- Bold colours with no shading, thick lines
- Each character has a signature colour that signals personality:
  - Hello Kitty's red bow = energy, approachability
  - Cinnamoroll's blue = calm, comfort
  - My Melody's pink = sweetness, gentleness
- Simplicity is the advantage -- makes characters infinitely adaptable

### 2.3 Translating Kawaii to Game UI

Based on research into kawaii product design and mobile game UI:

**Visual Principles:**
- Rounded corners on EVERYTHING (buttons, panels, cards, progress bars)
- Soft drop shadows (never harsh or dark -- use coloured shadows like rgba(pink))
- Pastel gradients instead of flat colours
- Generous whitespace (or rather, "pastel-space")
- Muted colours signal safety and calm
- No sharp angles anywhere in the interface

**Psychological Effect:**
- Kawaii design "promotes calm behaviour and narrows focus"
- Theorised to have "healing power" -- reduces stress
- Perfect alignment with "cozy game" goals
- Players associate soft visuals with a safe, no-pressure environment

**UI Element Guidelines:**
- Buttons: pill-shaped with soft gradient fills, gentle bounce on press
- Panels: rounded rectangles with 16-24px border radius
- Icons: simple, filled shapes (not outlined/stroked)
- Progress bars: rounded ends, pastel gradient fills
- Text: rounded sans-serif fonts, generous size
- Notifications: speech bubble or cloud shapes
- Dividers: dotted or dashed lines (never solid harsh lines)

### 2.5 Typography

**Recommended Fonts (Google Fonts, free):**

| Role | Font | Why |
|------|------|-----|
| Headings/UI | **Fredoka** | Round, bubbly, playful -- perfect kawaii energy. Variable weight. |
| Body text | **Nunito** | Rounded terminals, warm, highly legible at small sizes |
| Numbers/stats | **Varela Round** | Clean, rounded, excellent for scores and counters |
| Fallback | **Baloo 2** | Alternative bubbly heading font if Fredoka feels too heavy |

**Typography Rules:**
- Never use thin/light weights -- always medium to bold
- Letter-spacing: slightly loose (+0.5px) for warmth
- No ALL CAPS except for celebration text ("MAGNIFICENT!")
- Emoji should sit inline with text at 1.2x the font size

### Phase 2: Gameplay Polish (Core Feel)

**2.1 Merge Satisfaction (Steal from Travel Town)**

The merge moment is everything. Make it feel incredible:

**Screen Shake:**
- Current: 2px, 100ms for tier 6+
- Improved: graduated shake -- tier 4: 1px, tier 5: 2px, tier 6: 3px, tier 7: 4px, tier 8: 5px + haptic vibration
- Add a brief freeze-frame (50ms pause) on high-tier merges -- "hit stop" technique from fighting games

**Particle Burst:**
- Current: 8-12 small circles
- Improved: 12-20 particles of mixed shapes (hearts, stars, sparkles, circles)
- Particle colour matches the merge chain theme:
  - Flowers: pink/green particles
  - Fruit: orange/yellow particles
  - Crystal: blue/white particles
  - Butterfly: rainbow particles
  - Stars: gold/silver particles
  - Nature: green/brown particles
- Particles should have varied sizes and slight rotation

**Sound Design Direction:**
- Merge: ascending chime sequence (higher tier = higher pitch)
- Three-merge: same chime + sparkle overlay
- Generator tap: soft "pop" like a bubble
- Quest complete: music box melody (3-4 notes)
- Level up: harp glissando
- Background: lo-fi garden ambience (birds, gentle wind, water)
- Free sound sources:
  - Pixabay.com (royalty-free, no attribution required)
  - Audio.com Kawaii Sound Effects collection by Waveformwanderer
  - Uppbeat.io cute aesthetic collection
  - SFX Engine sparkle library

**2.2 Generator Improvements**

- Ready indicator: floating sparkles above generator (not just a glow ring)
- Tap feedback: generator does a little hop animation + produces item with a "pop"
- Cooldown: circular progress ring fills with pastel gradient, not just a grey overlay
- When cooldown completes: gentle "ding" + sparkle burst

**2.3 Board Interaction Improvements**

- **Drag preview**: when dragging an item, show a ghost/shadow at the target cell
- **Merge preview**: when hovering over a valid merge target, both items pulse with a soft glow and a "+" icon appears between them
- **Chain hint**: subtle connecting line (dotted, pastel) between mergeable items when board is idle for 10+ seconds
- **Auto-arrange suggestion**: after 30s of inactivity, gently highlight two items that can merge (optional, toggle in settings)

**2.4 Undo System**

- Already designed (10 coins) but likely not implemented yet
- Visual: "undo" button in bottom bar with a cute curved arrow icon
- Animation: items reverse-animate back to pre-merge positions
- Limit: last 3 actions (queue, not just one)

**2.5 Multi-Merge Enhancement**

- Current: three-merge produces next tier + bonus one tier below
- Enhancement: visual chain reaction -- items fly together in sequence with cascading particles
- Four-merge: next tier + TWO bonus items
- Five-merge: next tier + bonus at SAME tier (huge reward)
- Show a multiplier badge: "x3!", "x4!", "x5!" in bouncy bubble text

**2.6 Inventory/Storage**

- Off-board storage tray (3-5 slots) below the board
- Drag items to storage to free board space
- Stored items show a miniature version in the tray
- Free feature (not paywalled like competitors)
- Slots expand with level-ups

- Screenshots showing the kawaii aesthetic
- Price: free or $2.99 premium (no IAP)
- Target keywords: cozy, merge, garden, kawaii, relaxing, no ads

**4.3 Premium vs Freemium Analysis**

| Model | Pros | Cons |
|-------|------|------|
| **Free (current)** | Maximum reach, no barrier, gift-friendly | No revenue |
| **Premium $2.99-$4.99** | Clean experience, signals quality, indie-friendly | Barrier to install |
| **Free + cosmetic IAP** | Sustainable revenue, no gameplay impact | Slippery slope to predatory |
| **Tip jar** | Player goodwill, feels generous | Minimal revenue |

Recommendation: Stay free. This is a gift project. If it ever goes public, a one-time premium price ($2.99) with "no ads, no IAP, no energy -- ever" messaging is the strongest positioning.

## Part 4: Quick Wins (Highest Impact, Least Effort)

These can be implemented quickly and would dramatically improve the visual and gameplay experience.

### Immediate Visual Wins

**1. Colour Constants Swap (30 minutes)**
Replace all `COLORS` values in `src/utils/constants.ts` with the pastel palette from Section 2.4. This single file change transforms the entire game's look from dark/cyberpunk to kawaii/pastel.

**2. Font Change (15 minutes)**
Add Fredoka + Nunito via Google Fonts link in `index.html`. Update the `FONT` constant. Instantly makes all text feel warmer and rounder.

**3. Increase Corner Radius (10 minutes)**
Change `CORNER_RADIUS` from `s(10)` to `s(16)` or `s(20)` in constants. Every panel, card, and button becomes softer.

**4. XP Bar Gradient (15 minutes)**
Replace the solid XP bar fill with a two-colour pastel gradient (mint `#A8E6CF` to rose `#FFB3B3`). Single graphics call change.

**5. Pastel Cell Shadows (20 minutes)**
Add a soft shadow beneath each board cell (coloured shadow, not black). Use the cell border colour at 20-30% opacity, offset 0,2. Adds depth without harshness.

### Gameplay Feel Wins

**6. Merge Particle Colours (30 minutes)**
Current particles are white circles. Change to pastel-coloured particles that match the chain being merged (pink for flowers, orange for fruit, etc.). Update the particle emitter config per chain.

**7. Graduated Screen Shake (15 minutes)**
Add screen shake to tier 4 and 5 merges (currently only tier 6+). Lower tiers get subtle shake, higher tiers get more. Makes every merge above basic feel impactful.

**8. Merge Freeze Frame (10 minutes)**
Add a 50ms time.delayedCall pause after high-tier merges before particles burst. Creates a satisfying "beat" in the merge rhythm. Fighting game trick that works universally.

**9. Idle Merge Hint (30 minutes)**
After 10 seconds of no input, gently pulse two mergeable items on the board. Helps new players and reduces frustration. Subtle -- just a soft glow cycle, not an arrow.

**10. Generator Ready Bounce (15 minutes)**
When a generator's cooldown finishes, play a small bounce animation (scale 1.0 to 1.1 back to 1.0) in addition to the glow. More noticeable, more satisfying to tap.

### Polish Wins

**11. Ambient Background Particles (30 minutes)**
Add a Phaser particle emitter to the background scene: tiny floating sparkles/stars that drift slowly upward. Low count (10-15 particles), very subtle. Adds life to every screen.

**12. Button Press Animation (15 minutes)**
All tappable UI elements scale down to 0.95x on press and back to 1.0x on release with a 100ms ease. Makes every interaction feel responsive and juicy.

**13. Quest Complete Hearts (20 minutes)**
When a quest completes, change confetti particles from generic to hearts and stars. Fits the kawaii aesthetic and makes completion feel more rewarding.

**14. Combo Text Style Upgrade (20 minutes)**
Update "Nice!", "Amazing!", etc. text to use Fredoka Bold with a pastel-coloured outline/shadow instead of plain coloured text. Add a scale-up bounce animation (1.0 to 1.3 to 1.0).

**15. Soft Background Music Integration (45 minutes)**
Add a looping lo-fi/ambient track. Free resources:
- Pixabay has royalty-free ambient/garden music
- Single audio sprite with Phaser's sound manager
- Volume slider in settings
- Start quiet, fade in over 3 seconds
- Music pauses when app loses focus

## Summary

Merge Bloom already has strong game design fundamentals (merge chains, progression, quests, generous economy). The biggest opportunity is visual transformation. Swapping the dark colour palette to kawaii pastels, adding rounded fonts, and introducing a cute mascot would make the game feel like a completely different (and much cozier) experience.

The game's zero-ads, zero-energy, unlimited-gems design is genuinely unique in the merge game market. Every competitor frustrates players with energy walls and predatory monetisation. Merge Bloom can be the game that people wish Travel Town was -- all the satisfaction, none of the frustration, wrapped in an aesthetic that feels like a warm hug from Cinnamoroll.

**First session goal:** Items 1-7 from the quick wins list (colour swap, font, corner radius, XP gradient, cell shadows, background sparkles, button animations). Combined, these transform the entire visual identity in roughly 2-3 hours of work.
